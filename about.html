Ethereum is an open source, public, blockchain-based, distributed computing platform and operating system featuring smart contract functionality.

Like Bitcoin, Ether is a digital bearer asset - similar to a security, like a bond or trust, issued in physical form. Just like cash, it does not require a third party to process or approve a transaction. Instead of operating as a digital currency or payment, Ether seeks to provide “fuel” for the decentralized applications on the network.



Our app is the SMN token which acts as a public database for all our monetary transactions. When we issue a SMN token, it represents 10 cents on the USD. With 10 million in private circulation, these tokens are managed in digital wallets and, once distributed, the owner has full control of token management. When tokens are in private circulation, they cannot be manipulated and only one entity can distribute them from the source. The Salmon.Fund holds the source wallet with all 10 million tokens and contractually distributes them which creates a system of trust.

The value of an SMN token will never change based on 1 token = 10 cents USD. Every transaction can be publicly traced via blockchain with the added bonus of attaching data similar to the memo line on a personal check.

When recording a transaction on the blockchain, a small amount of money is used as a transaction fee. Ether refers to the microtransaction as gas and it represents 1/10 of a cent per transaction depending on speed. Gas is paid to crypto-miners for the cost of replicating ledgers as an incentive for their part in the validation process.

The Salmon.Fund chose Ethereum because of its speed and the security of its smart contracts. The security of blockchain technology is its cloud-based database of all transactions, duplicated millions of times across thousands of computers by the crypto-miners providing a verification system using sheer numbers. Token amounts can never be manipulated and the network is unhackable to date. A digital wallet is the only required software needed to hold SMN tokens; for usability and ease of entry, we direct our participants to Metamask.

All funds are distributed via Metamask in SMN tokens and withdrawals can be made after a request has been submitted and mailed via check to the withdrawers registered address. These requests can be in paper form or always accessible to the participant online at the Salmon.fund website. Annually the Salmon.fund performance will be evaluated based on project completion, material management, and participant withdrawal.

Excess funds exceeding 15% waste, will be redistributed to all wallet holders not exceeding 15% account interest.

Participants having permanent housing is linked to the accumulation of SMN tokens, a warranty deed, and Salmon.Fund properties. Micheal Salmon acts as the current owner of the house, the "grantor," and the “grantee” refers to the participant or trustee of the trust. The consideration is the amount of money paid for the transfer: $1 in the state of Kansas plus the square foot value in SMN tokens. The property's legal description will feature the address of the permanent home allocated to the participant. The deed will be notarized and, during the filing process at the county recorder’s office, a transfer tax form along with other necessary forms will be completed with the participant. The home will then be added to the trust agreement's property list with an attached, certified copy of the deed to complete the trust. All fees are paid for by the Salmon.fund.

As projects are completed, we will move qualified participants into our affordable homes at a fixed rate rent. This rent will be paid to the Salmon.fund through SMN tokens. Metamask is a mobile phone application designed to manage ether and ethereum tokens turning any mobile device into a digital wallet. Tokens are protected using a 12 word passphrase only known to the Participant and Salmon.fund for account support. Each Monday, tokens will be deposited into the digital wallets of each participant based on recorded labor hours. Our strategy for long term property maintenance begins here. Each participant that moves into our affordable homes will be required to complete the SMN Token Agreement which contains parameters for rent accumulation and the Home Ownership Agreement containing guidelines for property maintenance.



Salmon.Fund 501(c)3 focuses on three (3) primary revenue streams:
- Rent Accumulation
- Grants
- Public/Private Donations

On Dec 31st, all wallets holding SMN tokens will be given a 15% token increase based on total wallet value. This revenue share is based on projected participant program failure rates and partnership donations.
