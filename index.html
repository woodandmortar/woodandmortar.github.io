Look up the AFUE rating for the specific furnace model number or check the AFUE rating printed on the unit nameplate/rating plate attached to the furnace.

Input capacity - Furnaces over 225,000 BTU/hr input are required to be 90% AFUE minimum. So larger furnaces likely meet it.

Age - Units installed after 2015 generally meet 90%+ AFUE ratings due to federal standards. Older systems likely fall short.

Condensing - Check if the unit has a secondary heat exchanger and condensate drain. This indicates a high efficiency condensing furnace.

Multi-stage - Variable speed and modulating furnaces often indicate higher model series with 90%+ AFUE ratings.
